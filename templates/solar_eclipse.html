About Solar Eclipses

A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between the Sun and the Earth, blocking all or part of the Sun's light. This happens only during a new moon when the three celestial bodies are aligned in a straight line.

Types of Solar Eclipses

  • Total Solar Eclipse
  • Partial Solar Eclipse
  • Annular Solar Eclipse

Myths and Cultural Beliefs

Throughout history, solar eclipses have been the subject of various myths and cultural beliefs. Some cultures believed they were a sign of the gods' displeasure, while others saw them as opportunities for transformation.

Interesting Facts

  • A total solar eclipse is a rare event that can be seen from a specific location about once every 375 years.
  • The "diamond ring effect" is visible just before and after a total solar eclipse, when a small sliver of the Sun is still visible.
  • During a total solar eclipse, the sky becomes dark, and stars and planets become visible.
